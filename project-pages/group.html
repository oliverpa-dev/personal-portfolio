Introduction

Welcome to the breakdown of my contributions to the group project called Noise Control. Starting from 2023, a new law has been enacted which requires primary school students to engage in at least 2 hours of physical education every week. Consequently, physical education teachers will need to spend significantly more time in the gymnasium. However, some of these teachers already face issues such as tinnitus, hearing loss, and other problems associated with prolonged exposure to the gymnasium environment.

Empathize

Research results

  • Question: How can physical education teachers in primary schools get more insights into the sound levels and stress during their classes?
  • Research method: Ideation (Brainstorm)
  • Why: To generate and develop new ideas.
  • How: Initially, we began by distributing sticky notes to each person, allowing them to write down their individual ideas. Subsequently, we conducted a voting process to identify the most intriguing concepts. Once we had selected the noteworthy ideas, we proceeded to group similar ones together, consolidating them into clusters and ultimately condensing them into five distinct ideas. (Figure 1)
  • Results: As part of the Noise Control group project, several measures are being implemented to address sound-related concerns in the gymnasium. These include the introduction of a light indicator system that uses red and green colors to signify potential sound hazards and a healthy sound environment, respectively. Additionally, a sound meter is being installed to consistently monitor and measure noise levels within the gym. Furthermore, smart wristbands will be provided to individuals, notifying them when they have been exposed to prolonged high sound levels and reminding them to take necessary breaks or use earplugs as a precautionary measure. These combined efforts aim to promote a safer and more comfortable environment for physical education teachers and students. Read More

  • Question: Are there any existing measures for noise control in primary schools?
  • Research method: Literature Study
  • Why: : As the researcher, this approach grants me the opportunity to delve into the realm of existing knowledge, acquire contextual information, seek guidance, and uncover best practices within my field. Consequently, I can enhance my understanding and effectively apply valuable insights to the group's research.
  • How: I began by exploring the question I was given and conducted thorough research on the internet to find relevant literature pertaining to that question. Although it proved challenging to locate the necessary information, I persevered and ultimately succeeded. Subsequently, I gathered and compiled the insights that I deemed valuable and intriguing within the context of the project.
  • Results: In summary, various measures can be taken to improve acoustic conditions and reduce noise levels in classrooms. These include the use of acoustic ceiling tiles, carpets and rugs, acoustic panels, double glazing, sound-absorbing curtains, and sound-absorbing furniture. Acoustic ceiling tiles help absorb sound and minimize echo, while carpets and rugs contribute to sound absorption and noise reduction. Acoustic panels, when mounted on walls, effectively absorb sound and reduce noise levels. Double glazing helps to limit outside noise from entering the classroom. Sound-absorbing curtains and furniture with sound-absorbing properties also aid in reducing noise levels within the classroom environment. These solutions collectively aim to create a more conducive and quieter learning environment. Read More
  • Question: How are gyms in primary schools designed in terms of size, equipment, and location?
  • Research method: Document Analysis
  • Why: It facilitates a deeper understanding by engaging with an experienced professional who can offer valuable perspectives, knowledge, and recommendations aligned with the goals and objectives of the project.
  • How: Once we compiled the questions, we coordinated with our point of contact (POC) to secure an interview appointment. The interview was conducted by Majid, Wolf, and myself as a group.
  • Results: The stakeholder interview revealed several significant points. The primary goal identified was the improvement of sports halls by requesting manufacturers to create holes in the walls, particularly in the main sound reflective areas, for the placement of sound absorption materials. The second goal, which relates to the Grip op Geluid project, involved exploring the possibility of developing soundscapes to analyze and characterize activities with varying noise levels. Read More
  • Question: How can valuable insights be presented to the end users?
  • Research method: Competitor Analysis
  • Why: : It provides a thorough understanding of the applications within this market domain, encompassing the identification of key players, examination of their strengths and weaknesses, and gaining insights into their product offerings and strategies.
  • How: I selected and evaluated two sound applications, namely Sound Meter and Sound Tools SVM. I discovered them by conducting an internet search using the keywords "sound meter mobile apps" and came across a list with detailed descriptions. Both applications appeared to be pertinent and received positive reviews on both the Apple App Store and Google Play. I compiled the main insights and key takeaways, which are summarized below.
  • Results: In summary, the app provides a free and subscription-free solution with features such as a sound meter capable of measuring up to 100 decibels. It offers indicators for maximum, minimum, and average decibel levels, along with color-coded indicators for distinguishing healthy and unhealthy sound levels. The app allows calibration of the microphone and includes a graph to visualize sound levels over time. Users can save a short report summarizing recorded sound levels. However, the app has limitations, including a hard-to-understand navigation user interface, lack of information about microphone calibration, and a maximum limit of 100 decibels. It may not provide the same accuracy as professional sound measurement tools and fails to explain the potential harm of certain sound levels to human health. Read More

Watch apps

The Noise app on Apple Watch utilizes the device's microphone to measure ambient sound levels and exposure duration. When the decibel level reaches a threshold that could potentially impact hearing, the watch notifies the user through a gentle tap on the wrist. Apple suggests that the Apple Watch noise meter app generally aligns closely with proper noise meters during noise assessments. However, accuracy may be compromised when noise levels fluctuate significantly, causing the Apple Watch decibel readings to be less precise. Additionally, users can access more information about decibel levels by clicking on the info button, which redirects them to a separate page providing explanations on dB levels. Read More

Conclusion

Once the test was prepared and deemed ready, we expanded our participant pool to gather a larger volume of feedback, which could inform the development of a V2 version or guide our decision-making process. Following the test, we carefully analyzed the received feedback to extract valuable insights and actionable recommendations. The feedback revealed positive aspects of the prototype, such as its easy-to-read design and pleasing color scheme. Users appreciated the straightforward nature of the interface and found the inclusion of information about daily noise exposure to be valuable. The graph feature stood out as particularly useful, providing a clear understanding of the correlation between noise exposure and the time of day. Users also found it informative to see the decibel levels throughout the day and week, allowing them to identify potential areas of excessive noise exposure. However, there was initial confusion regarding the percentage values on the cards, which was resolved when users accessed the detailed graph. Some suggestions included adjusting the size of certain elements, such as the interface, to enhance the overall user experience. Overall, the feedback analysis highlighted the positive aspects of the prototype's design while also identifying areas for further clarification and optimization. Read More
