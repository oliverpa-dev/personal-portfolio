Introduction

Welcome to the breakdown of my contributions to the group project called Noise Control. Starting from 2023, a new law has been enacted which requires primary school students to engage in at least 2 hours of physical education every week. Consequently, physical education teachers will need to spend significantly more time in the gymnasium. However, some of these teachers already face issues such as tinnitus, hearing loss, and other problems associated with prolonged exposure to the gymnasium environment.

Research results

  • Question: How can physical education teachers in primary schools get more insights into the sound levels and stress during their classes?
  • Research method: Ideation (Brainstorm)
  • Why: To generate and develop new ideas.
  • How: Initially, we began by distributing sticky notes to each person, allowing them to write down their individual ideas. Subsequently, we conducted a voting process to identify the most intriguing concepts. Once we had selected the noteworthy ideas, we proceeded to group similar ones together, consolidating them into clusters and ultimately condensing them into five distinct ideas. (Figure 1)
  • Results: As part of the Noise Control group project, several measures are being implemented to address sound-related concerns in the gymnasium. These include the introduction of a light indicator system that uses red and green colors to signify potential sound hazards and a healthy sound environment, respectively. Additionally, a sound meter is being installed to consistently monitor and measure noise levels within the gym. Furthermore, smart wristbands will be provided to individuals, notifying them when they have been exposed to prolonged high sound levels and reminding them to take necessary breaks or use earplugs as a precautionary measure. These combined efforts aim to promote a safer and more comfortable environment for physical education teachers and students. Read More

  • Question: Are there any existing measures for noise control in primary schools?
  • Research method: Literature Study
  • Why: : As the researcher, this approach grants me the opportunity to delve into the realm of existing knowledge, acquire contextual information, seek guidance, and uncover best practices within my field. Consequently, I can enhance my understanding and effectively apply valuable insights to the group's research.
  • How: I began by exploring the question I was given and conducted thorough research on the internet to find relevant literature pertaining to that question. Although it proved challenging to locate the necessary information, I persevered and ultimately succeeded. Subsequently, I gathered and compiled the insights that I deemed valuable and intriguing within the context of the project.
  • Results: In summary, various measures can be taken to improve acoustic conditions and reduce noise levels in classrooms. These include the use of acoustic ceiling tiles, carpets and rugs, acoustic panels, double glazing, sound-absorbing curtains, and sound-absorbing furniture. Acoustic ceiling tiles help absorb sound and minimize echo, while carpets and rugs contribute to sound absorption and noise reduction. Acoustic panels, when mounted on walls, effectively absorb sound and reduce noise levels. Double glazing helps to limit outside noise from entering the classroom. Sound-absorbing curtains and furniture with sound-absorbing properties also aid in reducing noise levels within the classroom environment. These solutions collectively aim to create a more conducive and quieter learning environment. Read More

  • Results: In summary, the app provides a free and subscription-free solution with features such as a sound meter capable of measuring up to 100 decibels. It offers indicators for maximum, minimum, and average decibel levels, along with color-coded indicators for distinguishing healthy and unhealthy sound levels. The app allows calibration of the microphone and includes a graph to visualize sound levels over time. Users can save a short report summarizing recorded sound levels. However, the app has limitations, including a hard-to-understand navigation user interface, lack of information about microphone calibration, and a maximum limit of 100 decibels. Conclusion

In conclusion, our brainstorming session yielded several potential solutions for improving sound levels and stress management in primary school physical education classes. The idea of an interconnected ecosystem centered around a smartwatch app emerged as an intriguing concept. Our research process, guided by well-defined research questions, allowed us to delve into the problem from various angles and identify areas of opportunity. We explored solutions such as acoustic treatments, individually controlled noise-reducing devices, and expert recommendations for sports halls. Evaluating existing sound meter apps revealed their strengths and limitations, highlighting the need for improved user interfaces, calibration guidance, and education on sound-related risks. By combining these insights, we can shape a comprehensive solution that addresses the challenges faced by physical education teachers and promotes a healthier learning environment. Read More

Problem statement

The next step was to properly phrase a problem statement. This is a summary of the session. The TQ4 building at Fontys faces multiple challenges that can be summarized in two problem statements. Firstly, the educational institution struggles with organizing study profiles due to the absence of clear definitions, schedules, indicators, difficulty in finding classes, issues with funding teachers, and booking rooms. These problems hinder efficient study organization and create confusion for students and staff.

Secondly, the lack of a designated social area in the facility leads to various issues such as inappropriate congregation of smokers, insufficient space for rest, and inadequate provisions for lunch breaks. This negatively impacts the comfort, health, and overall well-being of individuals in the facility. Additionally, the current system lacks adequate provisions for privacy, neurodiversity suitability, and an overall plan to address these issues, creating challenges for individuals with different needs and preferences. By addressing these problem statements, the educational institution can foster a more organized, inclusive, and supportive environment for all individuals.. Read More

POV

Having insights from the previous problem statement session, we decided to use POV method in order to phrase the final statement. This is a summary of the results

In the context of human-centered design, the point of view for a student visiting the building 2-4 times a week involves two key needs: a clear definition of classes and activities in different areas and a sense of privacy, concentration, and an environment that fosters creativity. Insights from this perspective indicate that creating distinct zones within the building can address these needs effectively. By separating study areas from social or collaborative spaces, the building can provide a quieter atmosphere that promotes concentration and allows for focused work. This approach helps define specific activities, enabling students to easily identify suitable spaces for their needs while encouraging a conducive environment for creativity.

Prototype

Story line

The video begins by showing the school's entrance filled with students. The camera then focuses on a new student who appears confused and frustrated. However, their attention is captured by an information board, prompting them to approach it. By utilizing the school's facial recognition system, the student's face is quickly scanned, and their profile opens up. In the following scene, the video presents the school's dynamic windows, where the main character witnesses the transformation of the weather from rainy to sunny. As the video progresses, the student follows vibrant and colorful pathways that guide them to the classrooms. Finally, the video demonstrates the functionality of the school's virtual boards.
