Introduction

Welcome to our Design Challenge, where innovative thinking and practical problem-solving join together to tackle a crucial societal issue. By adopting a human-centered perspective, we aimed to develop revolutionary answers that have a beneficial influence on people and communities. In order to fully comprehend consumer needs and goals, we collaborate across fields and employ empathic design. We honed our ideas based on user feedback and ongoing learning using effective techniques like brainstorming and prototyping. Together, let's question the status quo, think creatively, and push the envelope of what is conceivable to build a future that is both forward-looking and inclusive.

Empathize

During the empathise stage of the design thinking process, we decided to start with a small brainstorming session regarding the most confusing thing about the TQ4 building.

The main topic that everyone picked was navigation throughout the building. Furthermore, we asked fellow students the same question and the result was the same, the navigation is the main problem from their point of view.

The next step was to create an empathy map in order to gain a deeper understanding of our users. Having finalised the empathy map it was time to create a persona of our target group which is students.

Problem statement

Black went through many shifts in meaning, application, and perception from era to era and culture to culture. Eventually, the color was revolutionized and given a prominent standing in the world of fashion, quickly becoming a symbol of elegance and simplicity.

As its name suggests, red-orange is a cross between the two hues. More red than orange, it is bright and exciting and associated with physical energy. It is playful, energetic, and engaging. Red-orange can also symbolize health and vitality.

Gray is a mixture of black and white. It is timeless and versatile, making it a great partner to a wide variety of colors. It is associated with cloudy days and, as a result, is not the cheeriest color. Gray, however, is often used in interior design to create a contemporary and sophisticated feel. Read More
