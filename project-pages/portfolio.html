01. Introduction

Personal portfolio is the most common way of collecting and depicting skills and projects that you have worked on. It helps during the potential job interviews to explain all the relevant projects that you are proud of.

This portfolio is created with the purpose of showcasing work and skills in a clear and meaningful manner for teachers, fellow students, and potential employers.

It will contain various sections, including an "About me" section providing a brief background story, a resume, and a projects section, depicting the journey throughout the "Advanced Media" semester.

02. First stage

02.1 Approach

During this project, I will use Trello for dividing my weekly sprints into descriptive to-do tasks. The research part will be conducted with the use of the dot framework methodology in order to get insights and validate my answers. Wireframe and the prototype will be created in Figma and the final development of the product will be done with the use of CSS, JavaScript, and HTML.

02.2 Research questions

  • What does the professional portfolio look like?
  • What kind of portfolio should a (front-end) web developer have?
  • What should a front-end portfolio contain?

02.3 Research methods

  • Competitive analysis → Investigation of different portfolios
  • Literature study → Find general information and the best practices
  • Brainstorm → Development of new ideas
  • Prototyping → Testing the concept (prototype website) before developing it
  • User testing → Evaluation of product with my peers and stakeholders
  • Validation → Peer, stakeholder feedback
  • Expert interview → Getting insights from Ux/Ui experts
  • Reflection → Advice and tips from teachers that I can reflect on
